A

Autonomous Structural Arbitration

A distributed correctness layer for modern storage systems

Reframing replica divergence as a correctness problem that can be resolved structurally — rather than primarily through controller authority, quorum workflow, or ad hoc reconciliation.

Problem shift

Redundancy is mature. Distributed correctness is not.

What storage largely solved

  • RAID, mirrored protection, and JBOD-plus-software stacks are standard deployment patterns.
  • VMware, cloud, and SDS platforms already assume replication, failover, and recovery primitives exist.
  • Most innovation now sits above the device layer, not inside the disk shelf.

What remains stubbornly hard

  • Replica divergence after partition, failover, or cross-site disruption.
  • Split-brain handling, resync ordering, and multi-cluster truth selection.
  • Operational debugging when metadata, controller decisions, and site state disagree.

Why now

Current systems still pay a coordination tax

Q

Quorum and witnesses

Stretch architectures use quorum or witness sites to preserve consistency and prevent split-brain, but availability and recovery behavior hinge on those coordination choices.

R

Resync and replay

After failover or outage, mirrored systems often require explicit resynchronization workflows to restore a consistent state.

O

Operational edge cases

Low-latency synchronous designs improve RPO but add inter-site latency sensitivity, replication overhead, and recovery dependencies.

ASA’s product thesis: reduce how much correctness depends on orchestration state, and increase how much can be inferred from the data structure itself.

Core mechanism

ASA determines truth structurally

Instead of asking which controller won, ASA asks which replica has the strongest structural evidence chain.

Structural metadata ties content to position, lineage, and mutation sequence.
Cryptographic hashes let the system detect where replicas diverge, not just that they diverged.
Deterministic arbitration logic selects the valid structure without relying exclusively on a centralized authority path.

Where it lands

Four buyers with immediate pain

VMware / Broadcom

Stretched-cluster failover, witness-loss edge cases, deterministic site recovery, and less manual intervention around post-failure object availability.

Nutanix / HCI

State reconciliation across nodes and clusters, especially where DR runbooks and platform metadata become hard to reason about under stress.

Ceph / cloud storage

Cross-site mirroring, snapshot consistency groups, and eventual or asynchronous reconciliation paths that currently depend on explicit resync behavior.

Enterprise array vendors

Cross-array federation, metro clusters, and controller-based replication stacks that need a software-native correctness differentiator.
