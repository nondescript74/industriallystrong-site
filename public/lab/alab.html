MoS₂ ALD on Tier-1 Platforms

As 2D materials move toward foundry integration, the limiting factor shifts from material performance to process stability within fixed tool architectures.

Context: MoS₂ RF switches for 6G — CVD monolayer transfer from growth wafers to target wafers using existing EBL tooling.
Bottleneck: Polymer residue from PMMA/EBL degrades MoS₂ contact interfaces. Serial EBL exposure damages monolayers via charging and radiolysis.
Implication: The patterning step must become an adaptive control layer — compensating for transfer-induced variability rather than enforcing static precision.

Five Process Modifications

Each modification replaces an EBL step with a VCSEL multiwriter equivalent — eliminating polymer residue and electron damage at the MoS₂ contact interface.

🔬

Contact Patterning: Replace EBL with VCSEL Direct Optical Write

Current (EBL)
EBL — serial, slow, electron dose (100–1000 µC/cm²) damages MoS₂ via charging and radiolysis
VCSEL Multiwriter
405 nm optical exposure avoids electron-induced damage mechanisms (charging, radiolysis, S-vacancy creation), shifting interaction into a controllable photochemical/photothermal regime
  • 405 nm at NA=0.7 → 290 nm resolution (sufficient for ≥300 nm contacts in far-field)
  • No electron-induced charging or radiolysis — categorically gentler than EBL
  • Future scaling path: near-field apertures (50–100 nm) for sub-diffraction contacts below 100 nm
📊

Dose Control: Shot-Noise Advantage of 405 nm Over EUV

Current (EBL)
EUV at 60 mJ/cm²: 163 photons per 2nm pixel → 8% shot noise
VCSEL Multiwriter
VCSEL 405nm at 20 mJ/cm²: 1,630 photons per 2nm pixel → 2.5% shot noise (3× improvement)
  • 3× better dose uniformity per pixel vs EUV
  • Lower contact edge roughness (LER/LWR) floor
  • Tighter filament formation zone → better ON/OFF ratio reproducibility
🧪

Resist Chemistry: Metal-Oxide or t-SPL Resists

Current (EBL)
PMMA leaves residue degrading MoS₂ contacts; AZ1512HS requires DUV + IBE cleaning
VCSEL Multiwriter
Metal-oxide resists (HfO₂/ZrO₂) — zero organic residue, 5–10× etch resistance; or gold mask lithography for contamination-free contacts
  • Zero organic residue after development
  • 5–10× higher etch resistance than PMMA
  • Gold mask lift-off protects MoS₂ surface throughout
  • Exposure calibrated against resist contrast curve (Hurter-Driffield) for stable CD control under local dose variation
⚡

Control Architecture: Local Analog Power Stabilization

Current (EBL)
FPGA loop latency 5–10 ns → 150–300× too slow for 33 ps pixel dwell
VCSEL Multiwriter
Per-VCSEL analog power stabilization (monitor photodiode + TIA) at the emitter level, plus slower zone-level compensation for transfer-induced thickness nonuniformity
  • 33 ps pixel dwell at 30 GHz per emitter — requires emitter-local analog control
  • Per-VCSEL monitor photodiode samples ~1% of output for real-time power stabilization
  • Zone-level dose correction compensates MoS₂ thickness variation across grain boundaries
  • Emitter-level power stabilization and scan strategies limit thermal accumulation and cross-talk during exposure
🔧

Transfer Process Integration: In-Situ Write in Transfer Chamber

Current (EBL)
Separate EBL patterning step with vacuum break, atmospheric contamination at contacts
VCSEL Multiwriter
VCSEL write head mounted in-situ in transfer tool — expose immediately after MoS₂ placement, before contamination
  • Zero vacuum break between transfer and patterning
  • No resist spin, no EBL chamber needed
  • Real-time compensation for MoS₂ thickness variation via per-zone dose feedback
  • Global alignment inherited from ALab's transfer tool; local exposure in continuous scan avoids stitching artifacts

Shot Noise Advantage: 405 nm vs EUV

At a 2×2 nm² voxel: EUV at 60 mJ/cm² delivers only 163 photons (8% shot noise). The VCSEL at 405 nm and 20 mJ/cm² delivers 1,630 photons (2.5% shot noise) — a 10× photon count and 3× noise reduction per pixel. This directly improves contact edge roughness and atomristor switching reproducibility.

Throughput: Emitter Scaling

Target: 60+ wafers per hour on 300 mm wafers. At 12,000 emitters running at 30 GHz each, total pixel rate is 3.6e+14 px/s. A full 300 mm wafer completes in 59 seconds (61 wph) including 20% stepping overhead.
Transfer Integration: The VCSEL write head mounts in-situ in the transfer tool. MoS₂ monolayer placement → immediate resist exposure → no vacuum break, no atmospheric contamination, no resist spin, no EBL chamber. Per-zone VCSEL dose feedback compensates for MoS₂ thickness variation across grain boundaries in real time.
