Control Requires Actuation Dimensionality

Distributed control is only as good as its actuation basis. 11 DOF is the minimum dimensionality to synthesize arbitrary PSFs under dynamic constraints — each writer head is an independent control node, not just a light source. Built from 2D planar semiconductor processes, each ~2x2mm die integrates the full actuation stack.

Why 11? Each degree of freedom maps to a specific control axis required for PSF synthesis under process drift. Spatial DOF (XY, Z, focus) position the beam. Temporal DOF (dithering, dose, coherent combination) shape energy delivery. Optical DOF (polarization, phase, wavelength) define the PSF. Thermal feedback closes the loop. Remove any one, and the controller loses the ability to compensate for a class of disturbance.

Each writer head is a semiconductor-fabricated 11-dimensional actuator. Die size: ~2x2 mm. Stack height: 137 µm. Fabricated using standard planar processes (CMOS, III-V epitaxy, PECVD, ALD). Batch yield: >1,000 heads per wafer run. Each head operates as an independent control node in the distributed array.

Conventional writer heads are passive optical elements. This head is an actuator — it compensates for local process variation in real time across spatial, temporal, optical, and thermal axes. Without this dimensionality, the control loop has no basis to correct.

Measured consequence: arbitrary PSF synthesis under dynamic constraints, per-head compensation for local process variation, and a control basis sufficient to maintain convergence as writer count scales.

11 Degrees of Freedom

The writer head is an 11-dimensional actuator. Spatial DOF (XY beam, Z stack, focus) define where the beam goes. Temporal DOF (dithering, dose integration, coherent combination) define when and how energy is delivered. Optical DOF (polarization, phase, wavelength) shape the PSF. Feedback (thermal) closes the loop for stability.
